Vikings

The Minnesota Vikings, established in 1960 and based in Minneapolis, are an NFL team known for their strong connection to the region's Scandinavian heritage. They quickly became competitive, reaching their first Super Bowl in 1969 under coach Bud Grant, and were famous for their fierce "Purple People Eaters" defense in the 1970s. Although the Vikings have made several Super Bowl appearances, they have yet to win a championship. They play at U.S. Bank Stadium, one of the NFL's most modern venues, and their passionate fan base is celebrated for the iconic "Skol" chant that energizes game days.
